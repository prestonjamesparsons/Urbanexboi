Torrington Dairy Crest


Urban Exploration - Torrington Dairy



Torrington Dairy Crest sits in Taddiport and was part of the dairy crest group. This site was huge and spanned over two buildings, one for processing and the other was for distribution. Walking inside this factory is an experience on its own from its Art Deco hallways and stairs to the small old fashioned clock outside, The ground floor is littered with Dartington Glass receipts and boxes but the top floor still held evidence to its dairy past.

We couldn't find any milk bottling machinery like at totnes but we did find other machines on the separator floor. We climbed the cooling tower (not advisable) to see the sights of taddiport although recently its become unsafe as its outside in the elements.

There used to be an old truck parked outside which was part of the dairy and had sat for many years but recently it has now gone

History of Totnes Dairy

The site of the Rolle Canal Company's stores was leased to Messrs Robert Sandford and Son, and there in 1874 began the Torridge Vale Butter factory, later to become the much expanded, highly mechanized Milk Marketing Board, Dairy Crest plant.

In 1993 the plant closed due to a fire on the site which damaged the drying plant and the deregulation of the industry left the structure of the plant remaining to dominate this part of the town and a huge deficit in employment opportunities.
